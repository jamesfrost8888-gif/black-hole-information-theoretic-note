# A Minimal Information-Theoretic Framing of Black-Hole Interiors

This note offers a concise, non‑technical framing of black‑hole interiors using information‑theoretic language. It is not a research paper, not a claim of novelty, and not an attempt to introduce new physics. It is simply a clean articulation of a structural idea for clarity and personal closure.

## 1. The Setup

A black hole can be viewed as a system that accepts classical inputs (matter, fields, boundary conditions) and evolves them into an interior state that is not directly observable from the outside. The exterior evolution is unitary; the interior evolution is inaccessible. The question is how to describe the interior without implying paradoxes or over‑committing to geometric pictures.

The framing here treats the interior as an information‑bearing subsystem whose structure is constrained by:
- the horizon as an information bottleneck,
- the no‑hair theorem as a compression rule,
- and the global unitarity of the full spacetime evolution.

This is not a model; it is a bookkeeping language.

## 2. The Interior as a Compressed Representation

The exterior state determines only a small set of macroscopic parameters (mass, charge, angular momentum). Everything else about the infalling matter is “compressed” into an interior representation that is not required to be geometric in the classical sense.

The key point is that the interior does not need to store a geometric history. It only needs to store enough structure to maintain global consistency with the exterior evolution. In this framing:

- The interior is a constrained information register.
- The horizon acts as a lossy interface for classical observables but not for quantum unitarity.
- The “singularity” is not treated as a physical location but as a limit of the compression map.

This avoids the need to imagine classical trajectories continuing past the horizon.

## 3. Interior Evolution as a One-Way Map

From the perspective of an infalling observer, interior evolution is smooth. From the perspective of an exterior observer, the interior is a one‑way map: information flows in but does not return in classical form.

This is compatible with:
- semiclassical gravity,
- horizon thermodynamics,
- and the idea that Hawking radiation encodes information only globally, not locally.

Nothing here requires specifying a microscopic mechanism.

## 4. No Paradox Required

The information‑theoretic framing avoids paradoxes by refusing to mix incompatible descriptions. The exterior description is unitary and complete for all exterior observables. The interior description is a compressed, non‑geometric register that maintains consistency but is not required to be readable or reversible from the outside.

This removes the need for:
- firewalls,
- cloning arguments,
- or geometric continuation beyond the classical domain.

It is simply a clean separation of descriptive layers.

## 5. Closure

This note is not a proposal, not a theory, and not an argument for any particular resolution of the information problem. It is a minimal, self‑contained way to talk about black‑hole interiors without over‑committing to geometry or metaphysics.

It is shared here only for clarity and closure.
